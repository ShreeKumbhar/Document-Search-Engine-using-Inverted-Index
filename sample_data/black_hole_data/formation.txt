The formation of black holes is a violent process. It usually starts with a star. A big star. Much bigger than the Sun. Stars fight gravity. They burn fuel. Nuclear fusion. Hydrogen to helium. Helium to carbon. And so on. This creates pressure. Pressure pushes out. Gravity pulls in. It is a balance. Hydrostatic equilibrium.

But fuel runs out. Fusion stops. Pressure drops. Gravity wins. The core collapses. If the star is very massive, nothing stops the collapse. Not electron degeneracy pressure. That stops white dwarfs. Not neutron degeneracy pressure. That stops neutron stars. The collapse continues. Down to a point. A singularity. A black hole is born.

This happens in a supernova. A giant explosion. The outer layers are blown off. The core falls in. If the core is more than 3 solar masses, it becomes a black hole. This is a stellar mass black hole. We see these in binary systems.

What about supermassive black holes? This is a mystery. They are too big to come from one star. Maybe they start as "seeds." Large gas clouds collapsing directly. Or huge stars in the early universe. Population III stars. They collapse. Then they grow. They eat gas. They eat stars. They merge with other black holes.

Mergers are important. When galaxies collide, their black holes merge. They spiral in. They lose energy. Gravitational waves. Finally they touch. A bigger black hole is made. The energy released is huge. Three solar masses converted to energy in a second. That is LIGO's first discovery. GW150914. Two black holes merged. 29 suns and 36 suns. Made a 62 sun black hole. 3 suns of energy gone.

Primordial black holes. These are hypothetical. Made in the Big Bang. High density pockets. Collapsed immediately. They could be tiny. Or huge. Some think they are dark matter. We haven't found them yet. But we look.

Let's focus on the collapse again. The iron core. Iron is the ash of fusion. You can't fuse iron and get energy. You lose energy. So the core dies. In a fraction of a second. It implodes. The shockwave blows up the star. Type II supernova. The remnant is the black hole.

TheTolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff limit. This is the max mass of a neutron star. About 2.2 solar masses. Above this, it must collapse. It becomes a black hole. Determining this limit is hard. We need to understand nuclear matter.

Growth of black holes. Eddington limit. There is a max speed they can eat. If they eat too fast, radiation pushes gas away. But they can cheat. Super-Eddington accretion. Maybe that's how they get so big so fast. We see quasars in the early universe. High redshift. Billion solar masses. Only 700 million years after Big Bang. How? It is a puzzle.

Intermediate mass black holes. IMBH. Between stellar and supermassive. 100 to 100,000 suns. These are the missing link. We found a few candidates. In globular clusters. Or ultra-luminous X-ray sources. ULXs. Maybe they are the seeds for the big ones.

The environment matters. Dense clusters help growth. More collisions. More mergers. Isolated black holes starve. They are dark. Hard to find. Maybe microlensing finds them. Gravity bends light. A star looks brighter for a moment. A black hole passed in front.

The future of black holes. The universe expands. Stars die. New stars stop forming. Galaxies separate. Only black holes remain. The Black Hole Era. Then they evaporate. Hawking radiation. 10^100 years. Then nothing. The heat death. A cold, dark finish.

But for now, they are active. They shape the cosmos. Feedback loops. AGN heat the gas. Stop star formation. They control the galaxy size. M-sigma relation. Example: Velocity dispersion of stars matches black hole mass. They are connected.

Spin is a property. Black holes have mass, spin, and charge. No-hair theorem. Only three numbers define them. Charge is usually zero. Mass and spin matter. Spin drags space. Frame dragging. Ergosphere. You can extract energy from the spin. Penrose process. Throw a rock in, get energy out.

Kerr metric. This describes rotating black holes. Schwarzschild metric is for non-rotating. Most real black holes spin. They come from spinning stars. Conservation of angular momentum. Imagine a skater pulling in arms. Spin speeds up. The collapsed core spins super fast.

Simulating this is hard. Numerical relativity. Supercomputers. Solve Einstein's equations. Predict the waves. Match with LIGO data. It works. The theory is solid. Einstein was right again.

The collapse of the core is driven by gravity. Gravity is the main driver. Without gravity, no black holes. Gravity overcomes all other forces. Strong force, weak force, electromagnetic force. Gravity wins in the end. It is the weakest force but it is cumulative. Big mass means big gravity.

Neutrinos are emitted during collapse. Lots of them. Most of the energy leaves as neutrinos. We detected them from SN1987A. That was a neutron star birth. But same idea.

Gamma Ray Bursts. GRBs. Short intense flashes. Maybe from black hole birth. Collapsar model. A jet punches through the star. Beamed at us. We see a flash. Very energetic.

So, formation is key. It explains where they come from. It explains their distribution. It is part of stellar evolution. The death of giants. The birth of monsters.
